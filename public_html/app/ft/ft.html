WORK JUST GOT EASIER

While I was employed at GE I was asked to help solve a problem: information within GE is very difficult to find. This is the story of how I used a sound research strategy to determine the root cause of the problem and develop a solution to solve the right problem for the user.

To comply with my confidentiality agreement I have omitted confidential information. Some designs are reinterpretations of the original

MY ROLE

I had the opportunity to conduct research into how GE employees obtained the information they needed in order to carry out their daily responsibilities at GE and then ideate a solution that would make that process more enjoyable and efficient. I independently conducted the research, ideation and testing myself and produced all of the artifacts shown in this case study.

CONTEXTUAL RESEARCH INTERVIEWS

It was common knowledge at GE that specific information, such as a certain form or application was difficult to find on the intranet. However, what wasn’t common knowledge was how different employees sought out that information, utilized it and produced it for others to find. Insight into those areas and more was what allowed me to create a solution users loved.

PERSONA DEVELOPMENT

Personas became a powerful tool when solving this problem because it helped me to identify that each member of the audience was looking to solve the same core problem, even if that need came from a different motivation. That insight helped me to create a solution that was intuitive and useful to the broadest audience possible.

A/B TESTING

The discovery phase suggested that there were two equally justifiable solutions to the challenge of finding information within GE: 1) Telling the user as much as we possibly know about their query in an organized fashion or 2) Give them the contact information of the person who is most likely to know the answer to their query. In order to find out which was most helpful in the majority of circumstances I developed a multivariate A/B test.

HIGH FIDELITY DESIGN & STYLE GUIDE

Once a design direction had been settled on based on the results of the A/B test and User Testing I created a High Fidelity Click-Through Mockup using Axure which showed all the expected interactions, tone and aesthetics.

the challenge

EMPHASIZE AND IDENTIFY

As UX Subject Matter Expert of the Innovation Studio Way Project Accelerator I had the opportunity to help other team’s leverage their unique skill sets to create amazing products that would improve the employee experience at GE. One such team had created an algorithm that was particularly good at taking input and creating fuzzy matches between instances within data. It was an awesomely powerful tool, but it didn’t have much of a purpose at the time within GE. Therefore, the team started to wonder if they could use this approach to improve the way that information was found in GE.

That question is where I came in. I was asked to research how employees were finding the information they needed to do their everyday jobs at GE and discover if there was a way that this team could make it better using their fuzzy matching algorithm.

I started by conducting open-ended contextual inquiry research with a variety of employees around the company in order to empathize with the challenges employees faced when trying to locate valuable information on GE’s Intranet.

the discovery

FINDING OUT JUST HOW BAD IT WAS

Discovery was run as a multiple week sprint where I conducted contextual inquiry interviews with prospective users and developed personas based on the research results. The magnitude of how much people were struggling to find information was incredible with employees reporting spending two or three hours a day looking for information with only a 50% success rate of finding the information they needed.

What was interesting was that there were several common trends across the results, which suggested that the system which was currently in place at GE was not supporting the way employees actually worked. These insights helped to shape the direction a new solution could take to empower users to find information the way they want.

While analyzing the contextual inquiry interview results I started to see that most queries could be generalized into two different buckets. The first was someone looking for “a starting point to an engagement” and the second, someone looking for “the person who knows X”. Given this I was able to create two primary personas, one for each case and one secondary persona to guide the design direction going forward.

the test

THE LAST NEWSPAPER GENERATION

The two primary personas seemed to be generally at odds, one was looking for a specific starting point on the intranet, the other the person who is most knowledgeable about the problem they were looking to solve. Generally speaking this came down to a preference, as both approaches would eventually lead to an answer, but which would better support the user and when?

In order to answer this question I developed two deep interaction mockups using Axure and developed a scenario based usability test where I asked participants to independently complete a series of open-ended real life scenarios using each the mockups. One mockup emphasized organizing the most information we could identify related to the topic of the query and the second returned the person within GE who was most closely related to the query topic.

THE RESULTS

EMPOWER THE USER

The results of the multivariate A/B test were clear, in some situations the contact information of a persona will always be more useful, sometimes the right information will be more useful and yet other times it will take both to locate the answer to a query. Therefore, the most successful solution would display both types of information in a clean, scannable manner that would empower the user to make a decision for themselves.

A CLEAR VALUE STORY

I designed the initial landing page to tell a story about how as a community of employees we could work together to build a better solution that would save millions of dollars and improve how we work.

THE CHOICE IS YOURS

The search journey was designed to fit the way that employees look for information. The search box is big, because employees typically know a lot about what they are looking for and users want to put more effort in up front by typing in longer quries rather than taking the effort later to scan through unrelvant results. Additionally, the more information the algorithm gets the more accurate its answer will be. The results page organizes a variety of information in a consistent and scannable way which enables the user to understand their results and then quickly take action.

CONTRIBUTING TO A GREATER CAUSE

In order for the algorithm to become even more effective I had to design a way to solicit feedback from users on how accurate their search results were. Also the design needed a way to gather mapping information so that the algorithm would have the contextual knowledge required to know which employees should be the answer to specific query topics.

THE IMPACT

READY SET ACTION!

The FIND IT Initiative was a success in GE because it fit the way that employees worked and provided them with actionable information they could use to solve real world problems at work. Instead of focusing on just building a better search engine, we were able to really understand how people were finding information in GE and why they were accomplishing their search in the ways that they were. Having that empathy for the user allowed us to design a solution that provided instantly recognizable meaning and filled unmet needs.
