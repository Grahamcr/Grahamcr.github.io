Supporting Takeaways:

Customers do not think in terms of single products, but in packages of products that will solve the situation or scenario they are currently facing

     - I need to host an application, does that mean I need a cloud service or a server? Does it come with network access or do I buy that separately?

      What about security, backup, domain names,  failover/load balancing, etc?



All Participants reported that they were unable to map products to product categories in the Current CoreTech Design

     - Design B Removes Abstract Product Category Mappings and Just Lists Products Under a Reduced Product Category List



While Design B participants reported it did a better job at supporting discovery, it still needed more focus on enabling decisions for first time customers

      - Additional success was most likely do the phrasing around the "Solutions" area, renamed as "Not Sure Where to Start" and the cleaner navigation scheme

      - Consider Taking this a step further and present customers with common package options or a guided shopping experience



Both designs failed to leave participants with a feeling that the site was anything more than a product catalog that might be more useful to skip and go directly to the source acquisition system.

      - Consider in-site purchases

      - Consider adding support information (tutorials, examples, etc)

      - Avoid ServiceNow whenever possible



Despite largely displaying the same information, 75% of Design B participants felt comfortable purchasing a product without having to contact someone at CoreTech (Self-Serve) compared to 25% of the current CoreTech Design. 

     - Actionable Information Presentation on Product Pages

     - Use of Comparison Chart

     - Clearly Perceived Purpose of CoreTech.ge.com



All Participants reported that Design B was easier to navigate and more comfortable for them to interact (both designs show the same products, just organized differently)

      - Additional Hierarchy/Breakdown of Products and Services

      - Combination of sections that make sense

      - Product-Centered Homepage



All Participants reported understanding CoreTech's purposed just based off of the homepage design and content when viewing Design B compared to none using the current CoreTech solution.

      - Clear explanation of products through graphic, tagline and product listing



The primary factor leading to a product purchase decision within a product category is comparison information and roadmaps

      - When users did not have a comparison chart, as shown in Design B, they resorted to opening all the product detail pages in different tabs to compare information

      - Users responded favorably to the roadmap shown in Design B and wished for more (Team, Product Category, Product and Organization)



Participants still struggled with Heuristic violations in the current CoreTech design

      - Right pointing arrows that scroll down to anchors

      - White text on images

      - Over use of images throughout site

      - Complex/hard to read layouts

      - Layouts which users perceive has being hard to respond to mobile platforms

      

Unrecoverable Issues  - Current CoreTech Design

Participants can't map product categories to actual products


Design feels scattered and inconsistent

     - Participants reported using the back button because they got lost and that the design "makes them think"


Customers report they are not able to find enough information to feel comfortable making a purchase

     - Missing quick access to key actionable data (product price, roadmap, availability, etc)

     - Missing comparative view of products within a product category


Design does not support discovery of products, only supports cases where you know what you need


Participants felt as though they were looking at lots of blocks of text


Purpose of the site is unclear to users


Products drop down is unmanageable/very hard to digest and use effectively


Images are nice at first, but eventually cause fatigue and too many decision points


Recoverable Issues - Current CoreTech Design

Homepage states three product categories, but has four boxes in the grid


Insights is a reserved word with a different meaning in GE


Participants weren't sure what the differences was between products and solutions


Participants would like a way to quickly contact the individuals on the Team Overview page


Participants didn't bother to read the About or Why CoreTech pages


Participants would like to see how products map to teams on the Team Overview page


Customers want to see their bill (out-of-scope)

Design B was more effective at enabling test participants to complete the main use-cases of the CoreTech site, but the feedback I collected in the process has revealed that in order for CoreTech.ge.com to be successful at enabling customers to identify and consume CoreTech products, the focus of the site needs to be on guiding customers to combinations of products and services that can be used to solve real-life IT challenges, such as setting up a hosting environment.
